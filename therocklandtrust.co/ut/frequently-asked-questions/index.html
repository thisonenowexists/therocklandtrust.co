Online Banking

  • Can I access my accounts from more than one computer?

    Yes. You can use any computer that has Internet access and a browser with 128-bit security. That means you can pay bills and access your account at home, at work, or from a laptop.

  • How current is the online statement information?

    When you connect to Online Banking, you will receive information on all of your account activity for the last two statements. The online information is current and displays all activity conducted through Rockland Trust Bank such as electronic deposits and cleared checks, plus transactions at ATMs and merchant point-of-sale terminals.

  • How do I enroll in Online Banking?

    You may enroll Online, or stop by any Rockland Trust Bank community banking office and ask a customer service representative for assistance setting up your account. You may also call us at (269) 371-8411 or (615) 499-6695 and we will assist you in the enrollment process over the phone.

  • How long does it take for bills to be paid online?

    It is necessary that all Scheduled Payment Dates selected by you be no less than five Business Days before the actual due date, not a late date and/or a date in the grace period. (For example, the payment should be entered no later than Monday before 8 p.m., Eastern Time [ET], for a payment to arrive on Friday.) Payment Instructions entered after the 8:00 p.m. ET Cutoff Time or on a non-Business Day will be considered entered in the Service on the next Business Day. If you properly follow the procedures described herein, and the Service fails to send a payment according to the Payment Instructions received, the Service will bear responsibility for all late charges (up to $50 per late payment). In any other event, including but not limited to choosing a Scheduled Payment Date which is not five Business Days before the due date or on or past the due date stated on your invoice or bill, the risk of incurring and the responsibility for paying any and all late charges or penalties shall be borne by you.

  • How much does it cost?

    Accessing account information through Online Banking is FREE to all personal account customers. Bill Pay comes FREE with your personal Online Banking account.

  • How secure is Online Banking?

    An outstanding feature of this system is its multiple security levels. Simply stated, Rockland Trust Bank’s Online Banking uses a combination of the latest security technologies to protect data. To get more technical, our system features password-controlled entry, authentication, and Secure Socket Layer (SSL) protocol for data encryption and state-of-the-art firewall protection. Regulation E applies to Online Banking just like our Debit MasterCard, and liability on unauthorized electronic transactions is limited to $50.00 for transactions that are reported in a timely manner.

    Please note: You will not be accessing Rockland Trust Bank's host computer when using online banking. When you request access for this service, your account information for Online Banking will be hosted on a separate server with all the appropriate firewalls and multiple layers of protection described above.

  • What can I do with Rockland Trust Bank’s Online Banking?

    You can check balances, review account histories, transfer funds between accounts, reorder checks, and issue stop payments. You will also have the option to use our Online Bill Pay service to pay bills. You can also communicate with Rockland Trust Bank through email.

  • What equipment and software will I need?

    You can access Rockland Trust Bank’s Online Banking from any computer that has Internet access. For best results, use the most recent version of one of the following browsers: Internet Explorer, Safari, Chrome, Firefox or Edge.

  • What if I forget my password?

    If you forget your Online Banking password, you can call us at (615) 499-6695 to have your password reset. You'll be asked to provide additional personal information that will verify your identification. Your password will then be reset and instructions to proceed will be sent to the email address that is on file with us. (It is very important to keep your email information up to date.) You will also have the ability to change your password online whenever you wish.

  • What is Online Banking?

    Online Banking lets you do your banking right from your PC at home or in the office. You now have access to all your banking information through the Internet. It's as easy as point and click. The safe and secure way to bank from your PC is available at Rockland Trust Bank. You can access our Online Banking services by clicking here. Simply click on the online demonstration to see how easy it is.

  • What accounts can I access through Online Banking?

    You may access any account on which you are an authorized signer. Checking, savings, money market, and loan accounts can be accessed through our Online Banking system. Transfers may be made between checking, savings, and money market accounts. You can even make payments on any loan through a transfer from your checking, savings, or money market account.

  • Which bills will I be able to pay through Online Bill Pay?

    You can pay almost anyone with a valid address in the United States -- from utilities to credit cards to your newspaper carrier. However, government regulations do not allow you to make payments for alimony, maintenance, child support, taxes, or other governmental fees and court-directed payments through this service.

  • What is Enhanced Authentication?

    As online fraud techniques have become more sophisticated, the Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council (FFIEC) has called on banks to make Online Banking access more secure. Rockland Trust Bank has taken a proactive approach to protect our customers by implementing a new Online Banking login process for personal and business accounts.

  • How does Enhanced Authentication work?

    In addition to your username and security login code, the new system recognizes your computer / device usage patterns. When it notices a questionable login attempt, or a login attempt on a new computer or device, it will prompt for additional information to authenticate the login request. If you are trying to access their accounts from a device outside of your normal usage, the system will attempt to verify that the device belongs to you and not a cybercriminal.

  • How is Enhanced Authentication more secure?

    Currently if a user logs into Online Banking and cannot remember a password, they are asked two of a set of predetermined list of questions. What the FFIEC has determined is that these questions are not nearly secure enough for today’s social media focused culture. The new authentication system will call or text customers, at the number we have on file, to ensure that the actual customer is the person making the request. If no phone number is on file or the user chooses “Security Questions”, we then ask a series of “Out of Wallet” questions from public records, which may include credit report data. As a reminder, the only option for the Mobile Banking platform is the “Security Questions.”

Personal Checking Accounts

  • Are the funds in my checking account FDIC-insured?

    All funds in a "-bearing transaction account" are insured in full by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation from December 31, 2010, through December 31, 2012. This temporary unlimited coverage is in addition to, and separate from, the coverage of at least $250,000 available to depositors under the FDIC's general insurance rules.

    The term "noninterest-bearing transaction account" includes a traditional checking account or demand deposit account on which the insured depository institution pays no interest. It does not include other accounts, such as traditional checking or demand accounts that may earn interest, NOW accounts, money-market deposit accounts, and Interest on Lawyers Trust Accounts ("IOLTAs").

    For more information about temporary FDIC insurance coverage of transaction accounts, visit www.fdic.gov.

  • How do I get a debit card (Debit MasterCard)?

    If you have already opened your Rockland Trust Bank Checking Account, you can request a debit card application by calling us at or by visiting any of our convenient community banking locations. Once your application is completed, it will take approximately 3 business days to receive your debit card by mail.

  • How do I re-order checks?

    To re-order checks, you have several different options.

    1. You can order on-line by clicking on the “Check Order” link in our Online Banking service and follow the easy do-it-yourself steps.
    2. Mail your re-order form found in your checkbook directly to Deluxe Checks or to us at Rockland Trust Bank.
    3. You can stop by any of our convenient community office locations and one of our employees would be happy to assist you.

ATM/Debit Cards

  • Can I use the Freedom Card (ATM/Debit MasterCard) for purchases only?

    Yes. While your Freedom Card allows you to make purchases as well as transactions at ATMs, you can request that your card is available to make purchases only. Please contact the nearest Rockland Trust Bank community banking office for assistance.

  • How can I get a Freedom Card (ATM/Debit MasterCard)?

    You can complete an application at any Rockland Trust Bank community banking location. And with our expedited issuance, you can get the card in just a few days.

  • How does an ATM/Debit Card work?

    An ATM/Debit card works like a credit card to make purchases wherever MasterCard is accepted. You can choose to sign for your purchase or enter you PIN. The card also works like a check, since all purchases are deducted from your primary checking account. And the card allows you to make ATM transactions -balance inquiries, withdrawals, transfers and more.

  • How is an ATM/Debit Card different than a credit card?

    While an ATM/Debit card is processed through the MasterCard system, it is not a credit card. The card works like a check - you're using your money. All debit card purchases are deducted from your primary checking account instead of accessing a line of credit as they would in a credit card purchase.

  • What account security measures are in place on my card?

    Your card has several security features. For example, you'll receive a Personal Identification Number (PIN) when you receive your new ATM/Debit card. This private number is coded to access your card number only and gives you the added security against anyone else using your card. It's just one more step we take to ensure your safety while you enjoy the convenience of using your new Freedom Card - ATM /Debit MasterCard.

  • How do I report a lost or stolen ATM/Debit Card?

    If you card is lost or stolen, contact the bank immediately at the appropriate number:

    • To report a lost or stolen Freedom Card (Debit MasterCard) during regular banking hours, call – (615) 499-6695
    • To report a lost or stolen Freedom Card (Debit MasterCard) after hours, call – (800) 236-2442

    Please visit the nearest Rockland Trust Bank community banking location or call (615) 499-6695 to request a new card.

  • Can I use my ATM/Debit Card in a foreign country?

    Yes, you can use your debit card while traveling outside the United States. However, to prevent fraud certain countries have been blocked. Please contact our data operations/debit card processing area to ensure that your card will be accessible in the countries that you are planning to visit.

  • My ATM/Debit Card is going to expire soon. Do I need to to anything?

    You should receive your new card in the mail about 2-3 weeks before your current card expires. Your card is valid through the end of the month that appears on the front of your card. If you don't receive your new card within two weeks of your card's expiration, or if your card has already expired, please call us at (615) 499-6695.

  • How do I get a replacement for a damaged ATM/Debit Card?

    If your ATM/Debit Card is damaged or not functioning properly, you may order a replacement card. Please visit the nearest Rockland Trust Bank community banking office or call us at (615) 499-6695. You will receive a new card within 5-7 business days. Or, you may pick-up your new card within 2 business days at an Rockland Trust Bank office through expedited issuance. You can continue to use the same PIN you are currently using with your damaged card.

Switch Kit

  • What is a Switch Kit?

    A Switch Kit is a free service that allows you to close an account with another financial institution and transfer the funds to a new account (this account must already be set up). You can also switch any direct deposits and automatic payments (ACH) from your old account(s) over to your new account(s). The Switch Kit contains a series of easy to follow steps, along with the necessary forms to fill out and mail to all respective parties.

  • Who can use the Switch Kit?

    Anyone who wants to switch funds from one financial institution to Rockland Trust Bank.

  • Do I have to be a customer to use the Switch Kit?

    Yes, you do have to be a customer so that we know where to send all of your payments, debits and funds.

  • I can't find my account number on my check, where is it?

    Your account number is located at the bottom of your check. The routing number is the first number listed and it is 9 digits. The second set of numbers is your account number. The last number listed is the actual check number.

  • What information will I have to know when filling out the Switch Kit?

    You will need to know the following:

    1. Your current account number where you are transferring the funds from.
    2. Your new account number where you are transferring the funds to.
    3. Name, Address, Account Number of Direct Deposit and Automatic Payment (ACH) companies that you currently are using.
  • Do I have to have a Direct Deposit or Automatic Payment (ACH) to fill out the Switch Kit?

    No, you can simply just close out a current account and move the funds to Rockland Trust Bank. The Direct Deposit and Automatic Payment (ACH) transfers are optional.

  • What do I do with the printed forms?

    All the forms are clearly marked and you are also given a check list to assist you with the forms.

  • What do I need in order to view the forms?

    The forms are displayed in Adobe PDF format. You can download a free version of this application in order to view the forms. If you do not have Adobe Acrobat Reader installed on your computer, you will need to download the reader.

  • Who can I contact if I need help?

    Please contact Rockland Trust Bank with any questions at (269) 371-8411 or toll free at (615) 499-6695.

  • Is my information secure?

    Yes, your information is encrypted with 128 bit encryption.
